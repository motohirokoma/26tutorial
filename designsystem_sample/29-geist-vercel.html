Brand documented

Vercel · Geist

Geist is Vercel's design system. The aesthetic is unapologetically monochromatic: pure black and pure white anchor the system, with a 9-step gray scale providing the entire grayscale information hierarchy. Vercel blue, pink, and cyan are reserved as accent moments — never decoration.

Monochrome-first

Pure black on pure white. Color is a feature flag, not a default.

Editorial typography

Geist Sans was designed for code and editorial. Tracking is tight, weights are intentional.

High contrast

Strong borders, hairline strokes, and crisp 5-8px corner radii. No gradients in core UI.

Open source

Geist Sans + Mono are released under SIL OFL. Geist Icons are MIT.

Buttons documented Official ↗

Geist's buttons are unapologetically minimal — pure black primary, hairline secondary, and a tertiary that's barely there. 5px corner radius is one of the system's signatures.

Surfaces documented Official ↗

Cards in Geist favor flat, hairline-bordered containers over shadows. The inverted (black-on-white-on-black) card is a signature pattern, as is the violet-to-pink gradient card from the Vercel marketing surface.

Cards

Default card

Hairline border, 12px corner radius. The default surface across product UI.

Elevated card

Subtle shadow only. Geist almost never uses heavy elevation.

Inverted card

Pure black surface. Used for emphasis on light pages, footers, and CTAs.

Gradient card

Vercel's signature violet-to-pink gradient. Marketing surfaces only.
