Brand documented

USWDS

The U.S. Web Design System (USWDS) is the open-source toolkit used across federal government websites. Its visual language is deliberately civic: bold blue (#005EA2) as the primary anchor, sharp 0px corner radii, generous 17px body type, and an accessibility-first posture that treats WCAG conformance as a default, not a feature.

Accessible

WCAG 2.1 AA conformance is built into every component, not bolted on.

Federal

Sharp shapes, civic blue, and the ever-present "Official site" banner.

Public-domain

Released under CC0 — every agency can adopt without licensing friction.

Plain language

Form labels, helper copy, and error messages are written for everyone.

Typography documented Official ↗

USWDS uses a paired typeface system. Source Sans 3 is the body and heading default; Public Sans is the alternate sans (used by .gov agency sites such as veterans.gov); Roboto Mono for code. Body sets at 17px with 1.5 line-height — generous for civic readability.

Buttons documented Official ↗

USWDS buttons sit at 17px / weight 700, padding 12×20, and 4px corners. The 4px focus ring is cyan-30v — required for accessibility. Accent-cool and accent-warm buttons reference USWDS's secondary and tertiary callouts.

Form documented Official ↗

Inputs are bordered (1px base, 4px error). Labels sit above the input as bold black text. Hint text follows below in muted gray. The required-field asterisk uses red-warm-60v.

Feedback documented Official ↗

USWDS Alerts use an 8px left bar in the semantic color, a tinted background, and an icon. The "Emergency" alert is unique to USWDS — solid emergency color (#9C3D10) for federal incident communication.
