Brand inspired

Slack

Slack's design balances enterprise reliability with playful friendliness. The signature aubergine anchors a 4-color palette of yellow, sky, mint, and pink — each a distinct hue, applied generously to convey approachability inside professional software.

Friendly

A 4-color palette and Lato's warm geometry soften the workplace tool into something people enjoy opening.

Focused

Channel structure and message threads guide attention; chrome stays out of the way of conversations.

Reliable

Density, contrast, and predictable affordances ensure Slack works under heavy daily enterprise use.

Expressive

Emoji, custom statuses, and personalized themes let teams shape Slack to their culture.

Color inspired Official

The aubergine purple anchors Slack chrome; the 4-color set (Yellow, Sky, Mint, Pink) is reserved for emphasis, illustration, and signature moments.

Typography inspired Official

Lato (Google Fonts) is used as the primary brand typeface — warm humanist sans with strong italics. Weights: 400 / 700 / 900.

Buttons inspired Slack.com

Slack's primary CTA uses Mint Green (#2EB67D) — a confident, approachable affirmative. Danger uses the signature pink. All buttons are 900 weight.

Slack's primary navigation lives in the dark left sidebar with channel groups; the top bar carries search and actions. Tabs and breadcrumbs use the body-light surface.
