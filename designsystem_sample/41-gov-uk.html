Brand documented

GOV.UK

The GOV.UK Design System helps teams build accessible, consistent services across UK central government. It prioritises plain English, high contrast, and progressive enhancement — designed to work for the widest possible audience including users on slow connections, low-spec devices, and assistive technology.

Accessible by default

WCAG 2.2 AA conformance is the floor, not the ceiling. Every component is designed for keyboard, screen reader, and zoom users.

Plain & honest

Designs are text-first. Decorative imagery and animation are avoided unless they help the user complete a task.

Tested with users

Components have been tested with real users including assistive technology users, in real services, in real conditions.

Colour documented Official ↗

GOV.UK uses a small, deliberate palette. Black on white sets the baseline. Blue identifies links and primary actions; yellow is reserved exclusively for the focus state.

Buttons documented Official ↗

Buttons are green by default to indicate the primary action. They have a 2px hard shadow that disappears when pressed, mimicking a physical key. There is no border-radius.

Form documented Official ↗

One question per page is the GOV.UK form rule. Inputs are large (40px+), high contrast (2px black border), and labels are placed above the field. Error states use a 4px red bar.

Layout documented Official ↗

GOV.UK uses a fluid 12-column grid with a 30px gutter, capped at 1100px. Content columns are typically two-thirds (760px) for prose to keep line lengths comfortable.

Radius scale (sharp only)

GOV.UK does not use rounded corners. Buttons, inputs, banners, and panels are all sharp-cornered to maintain a deliberate, plain aesthetic.

Shadow scale

none (default)
button (2px)
focus

GOV.UK uses shadows extremely sparingly: a 2px hard shadow on buttons (to suggest a key) and a 4px black underline on focused links / buttons over the yellow focus background.

Focus state

Yellow background (#FFDD00) with a 4px solid black underline. Required on every interactive element.
