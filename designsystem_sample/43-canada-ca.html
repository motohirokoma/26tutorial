Brand documented

C Canada.ca

The Canada.ca Design System unifies the digital experience of every Government of Canada service. Rooted in the Federal Identity Program (FIP), it places bilingual content, accessibility, and trust above visual flourish. Pages are clear, conservative, and designed to work for every citizen on every device.

Bilingual

Every interface ships in English and French as a constitutional requirement, not an afterthought.

Accessible

WCAG 2.1 AA is the floor. High-contrast palettes, 44px touch targets, visible focus.

Trustworthy

FIP blue and conservative typography signal an official Government of Canada service.

Service-first

Patterns are written for transactional flows: applying, paying, renewing.

EN

"Apply for a passport — get the documents you need to travel internationally."

Accessibility checklist

  • ✓
    Contrast ratio
    All body text passes WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1) and large text passes AAA (7:1) on the FIP palette.
  • ✓
    Touch targets
    Minimum 44×44px for any clickable element on mobile.
  • ✓
    Focus visible
    A 3px solid #FFBF47 ring marks the keyboard-focused element on every interactive surface.
  • ✓
    Bilingual labels
    Every label, hint, and error has been translated. EN and FR strings ship together at the page level.
  • ✓
    Semantic HTML
    Use native <button>, <label>, <fieldset>, <legend>. No div-buttons.

Patterns & voice documented Official ↗

Canada.ca content is plain language at a Grade 6–8 reading level. Tone is calm, factual, and second-person ("you"). The bilingual page model places EN and FR side-by-side at the URL level (not inline).

Voice & tone

Plain

Use simple words. "Use" not "utilize". Short sentences. Active voice.

Style guide →

Direct

Tell the user what to do, in order. "Sign in. Choose a service. Confirm your details."

Respectful

No jargon, no acronyms on first use, no marketing language. The Government of Canada speaks to citizens, not customers.

Bilingual-first

Write content with translation in mind. Avoid idioms, puns, and metaphors that don't survive crossing languages.

FAQ pattern

How long does it take to receive a passport?
Standard online renewals take 10 business days. Urgent and express services are available for an additional fee.
What documents do I need?
Your prior passport, two passport-style photos, and proof of Canadian citizenship if applying for the first time.
Can I track my application?
Yes — sign in to your Canada.ca account and select "My applications" to view status updates.

Brand do / don't

Do

Use the FIP wordmark only on the official Canada.ca chrome — header and footer.

Do

Pair English and French at the URL level — `/en/` and `/fr/` mirrors, not inline.

Don't

Re-colour the FIP blue or apply gradients. The signature blue is non-negotiable.

Don't

Use marketing slogans, exclamation marks, or emoji in service copy.
